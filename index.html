Everyone seems to have a website now, so I thought I'd make one too. I'm Sunny, and I'm looking to move into helping with the efforts to keep AI safe.

I recently finished my Physics PhD at Oxford, working with lots of machine learning to capture the structure of ultra-intense laser pulses. If anyone wants to work on this intersection I would massively recommend my supervisors, Andreas and Peter. Before this, I did a short time in Cambridge working on nanophotonics and studied Physics and Machine Learning at Nottingham (BSc and MSc respectively).

During my MSc I read some books on the impact of superintelligent AI, like Life 3.0 and Human Compatible. It seemed very important, but also a problem far in the future. In the last 5 years, LLMs have gone from struggling to make coherent sentences to being able to answer physics problems better than PhD students (yikes). At the same time, they are already exhibiting misalignment, like deception. Thus, I feel that one of the most valuable things one can do is to work to ensure that AI is beneficial for all beings.
